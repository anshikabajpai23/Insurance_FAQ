# 🛡️ ClaimSmart – Your Insurance Claims Assistant

## 🚗 How much car insurance do I need?

How much car insurance you need depends on the coverage your state or lender requires and how much financial risk you want to protect against. Liability limits, vehicle coverage, and medical coverage all play a role in how much protection makes sense for you. The right amount comes down to what you own, what you owe, and how much you could afford to pay out of pocket after an accident.

## ⚖️ What is liability car insurance?

Liability coverage pays another party's medical expenses, vehicle repairs, and property damage if you were responsible for the accident. It does not cover your own vehicle or your own injuries.

## 🔧 What is collision coverage?

Collision coverage helps pay to repair or replace your vehicle if it overturns or collides with another vehicle or object, such as a tree or guardrail.

## 🌧️ What is comprehensive coverage?

Comprehensive coverage helps pay to repair or replace your vehicle if it's damaged by something other than a collision, including theft, fire, vandalism, or hitting an animal.

## 🤔 Is collision or comprehensive coverage better?

Collision and comprehensive cover different types of losses. Collision applies when your car hits or is hit by another vehicle or object. Comprehensive applies for non-collision events like theft, weather, or animals. Both are recommended and the right choice depends on your individual needs and vehicle value.

## 🛣️ What is full coverage car insurance?

Full coverage is not a formally defined insurance term, but it typically refers to having both liability and physical damage coverage, including comprehensive and collision coverage.

## 🏥 What is Medical Payments coverage?

Medical Payments coverage helps pay medical and funeral expenses if an insured person, including a passenger, is injured or killed in an accident, regardless of who is at fault.

## 🩺 What is personal injury protection (PIP)?

Personal injury protection, or PIP, covers medical expenses, lost wages, and other costs if you are injured in an accident, regardless of fault. PIP typically applies before your health insurance. The amount of PIP coverage you need depends on your health insurance and financial situation. If you have good health insurance, your state's minimum PIP limits may be adequate. Fifteen states currently mandate PIP coverage, while others offer it optionally.

## 🚘 What is uninsured and underinsured motorist coverage?

Uninsured motorist coverage helps pay for your medical expenses, pain and suffering, and lost wages when you are hit by a driver who has no insurance. Underinsured motorist coverage applies when the at-fault driver has insurance but not enough to cover your total damages.

## 🔁 What is a third-party insurance claim?

A third-party insurance claim is a claim you file with someone else's insurance company after they cause an accident. If another driver hits you on the road, you can file a third-party claim with their insurer to help cover your injuries and damaged property. These may include vehicle repairs, medical bills, or other covered costs, depending on the other driver's liability coverage and limits. If you cause an accident, your liability coverage may help pay for third-party claims against you.

## 🌊 Does car insurance cover water damage?

Comprehensive coverage can protect your vehicle against water damage caused by flooding, heavy rains, hail, and even tree branches blown down during a storm. Comprehensive is the only coverage on your auto policy that can cover your vehicle against weather-related issues, and a deductible applies. Coverage cannot be purchased after a major storm has been forecasted.

## 🔨 Does car insurance cover vandalism?

Yes, comprehensive coverage on your auto policy can cover vandalism to your car — minus any deductible — since intentional damage to your vehicle is out of your control. Vandalism includes slashed tires, broken windows, and any type of defacing of the vehicle, like a car that gets keyed. If the repairs cost more than your comprehensive deductible, it may make sense to file a claim.

## 🔩 Does car insurance cover tire damage?

Car insurance may help cover tire damage if it's caused by a covered event, such as an accident, vandalism, theft, or severe weather, and you have comprehensive or collision coverage. However, tire damage from normal wear and tear, nails, or flats without a covered cause typically isn't covered, though a manufacturer's warranty or a tire protection plan may offer additional protection in some cases.

## 🚫 What is an excluded driver on a car insurance policy?

An excluded driver is a person in your household who has been explicitly excluded from coverage under your car insurance policy. Their name will show as excluded on your policy, and they won't be insured to drive any vehicles on your policy. Not all states allow policyholders to exclude household members from coverage. Accidents involving excluded drivers won't be covered.

## ➕ Can I add a driver to my car insurance?

Yes. Most insurance companies allow you to add another driver to your car insurance policy if the person drives the insured cars regularly or shares the same permanent residence. You can typically make a change to your policy at any time to add a new driver. To add someone, contact your insurance company by phone or online. Your insurer will typically need the driver's name, date of birth, driving history, and license information.

## 📄 What is an SR-22?

An SR-22 is a form that proves your car insurance meets the minimum coverages required by law. It is also known as a Certificate of Financial Responsibility. Courts or states typically require an SR-22 following serious infractions like driving without a valid license, driving without insurance, or a DUI or DWI conviction. To get an SR-22, contact your insurance company and they will file it with your state. An SR-22 costs approximately $25 to file, though the cost may vary by state and insurance company.

## 🚨 What should I do if I'm hit by an uninsured driver?

Check for injuries and call for medical help. Report the accident to police. Exchange information with the other driver. Avoid informal payment agreements. Notify your insurance company promptly. Several coverages may apply, including uninsured motorist bodily injury, uninsured motorist property damage, collision, and medical payments coverage. Keep copies of medical bills, treatment plans, and records of time missed from work. Approximately 15% of motorists were uninsured in 2023.

## 📞 How do I report a claim or glass damage?

You can report a claim through your insurer's website, mobile app, or by calling their claims line. Many insurers also have separate portals for glass damage and roadside assistance requests.

## 🔍 How do I find my coverage and deductible for a claim?

Visit the Coverage Section of your insurer's website or app to review your vehicle coverage. The type of incident will determine how your coverage or deductibles apply.

## 📲 How do I track and manage my existing claim?

Most insurers allow you to track your claim online or through their mobile app using your name and claim number. You can also contact a Claims Service Representative directly for updates.

## 🛠️ Where can I get information on repair shops after an accident?

Access your claim online through your insurer's website or mobile app, or contact a Claims Service Representative for a list of approved repair facilities in your area.

## 📋 Where can I view online claims forms?

Visit your insurer's Claims Center on their website to find medical release forms, accident description forms, and vehicle questionnaires.

## 💸 What is Auto Pay and how does it work?

Auto Pay is a payment option that automatically withdraws a scheduled payment on your due date. At each policy renewal or change, your insurer will send a payment schedule so you can plan for each payment. Benefits include never missing payments and avoiding the need to mail checks.

## 🏦 What are the different ways I can make an insurance payment?

Options typically include automatic payments from a bank account or card, online payments through the insurer's website, a mobile app, paying by phone, a bill payer service through your bank, or mailing a check to the insurer's remittance center.

## ⏳ Can I postpone my Auto Pay payment past the due date?

You may have the ability to postpone your next scheduled payment. Log in to the Billing Summary page of your insurer's account portal. If eligible, a Pay Bill Later option will be available under the Upcoming Payments section. Postponement may be unavailable in certain circumstances.

## ⚠️ Why did I receive a cancellation notice?

Cancellation notices are sent when your payment due date has passed and the amount due has not been paid. They indicate the final deadline to make a payment before your coverage lapses.

## 🚘 Is there a discount for being accident-free?

You may be eligible for a good driver discount as long as you have not had any accidents or moving violations that have resulted in a premium surcharge. Eligibility requirements vary by insurer and state.

## 🎓 Is there a Defensive Driver discount?

Many insurers offer a discount for completing an approved defensive driving course. Availability varies by state — check with your insurer for your state's specific requirements and which courses qualify.

## 🔒 What discounts are available for vehicle safety features?

Discounts typically apply to vehicles equipped with anti-lock brakes, anti-theft devices, daytime running lights, and airbags. Availability and discount amounts vary by state and the specific coverage on your policy.

## 🏠 What does homeowners insurance cover?

Standard homeowners insurance covers damage to your home's structure from covered perils such as fire, windstorm, hail, and lightning. It also covers personal belongings, liability if someone is injured on your property, and additional living expenses if you need to temporarily live elsewhere while your home is being repaired.

## 🏚️ Is homeowners insurance required by law?

It varies by state whether the law requires you to carry homeowners insurance. However, your mortgage lender will typically require certain coverages and amounts in order to issue a loan, because the home serves as collateral for the loan and insurance protects the lender's financial interest if the property is damaged or destroyed.

## 🌧️ What is hazard insurance and is it different from homeowners insurance?

Hazard insurance is part of your homeowners policy and does not need to be purchased separately. Hazard insurance covers the dwelling from roof to foundation, but not your home's contents.

## 🌊 Does homeowners insurance cover flood damage?

Standard homeowners insurance policies do not cover flood damage. Flood coverage is provided through a separate flood insurance policy available through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or private insurers. Most flood insurance is written through the NFIP, which is administered by the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA).

## 🌍 Does homeowners insurance cover earthquake damage?

No. Earthquake damage is not covered by standard homeowners insurance. You must purchase a separate earthquake insurance policy or endorsement. The ground can shake in almost any state, so this coverage is worth considering depending on your location.

## 🐕 Can I get homeowners insurance if I have a dog?

Yes, you can be eligible for homeowners insurance if you have a dog. However, some providers may have exclusions or restrictions for specific breeds or dogs with a history of aggression.

## 💧 What kind of water damage does homeowners insurance cover?

Homeowners insurance covers sudden and accidental water damage. This can include situations like a washing machine overflowing, a pipe bursting, or water entering the home due to a storm damaging the roof or windows. It typically does not cover water damage from external flooding, gradual damage, or unresolved maintenance issues.

## 🔧 Does homeowners insurance cover plumbing issues?

Homeowners insurance typically covers plumbing issues if the damage is sudden and accidental — for example, if a pipe bursts unexpectedly and causes water damage to your home. However, it does not cover plumbing issues caused by negligence or lack of maintenance, such as slow leaks, corrosion, or normal wear and tear.

## 🧫 Does homeowners insurance cover mold damage?

Mold damage coverage varies by policy. Standard homeowners insurance policies typically do not cover mold damage caused by preventable issues such as lack of maintenance or water leaks. However, if mold results from a covered peril such as a burst pipe, coverage may be available for the resulting damage but not for the mold remediation itself.

## 👜 Are personal belongings outside the home covered?

Many policies include off-premises coverage, which means items outside of your home may be covered against the same disasters listed in your policy. However, there is typically a limit on the amount your insurer will reimburse you.

## 🏢 Does homeowners insurance cover a condo?

Standard homeowners insurance covers the interior and exterior of a house. Condo insurance covers your interior, not the structure. Check your condo association bylaws to determine the type of coverage required for your unit.

## 🏕️ Does homeowners insurance cover a mobile home?

Most homeowners policies do not cover a mobile home. Mobile and manufactured homes require different types of coverage. Many insurers offer a distinct manufactured home product for these situations.

## 💰 How can I save money on my homeowners insurance?

To save money on homeowners insurance, consider bundling your home and auto policies, installing home security systems, and maintaining a good credit score. You can also explore discounts for a claim-free history and other qualifying factors available through your insurer.

## 🧾 Is homeowners insurance tax deductible?

In most cases, homeowners insurance premiums are not tax deductible for a primary residence. However, there may be exceptions if part of your home is used for business purposes or if the property is a rental.

## 🚗 What is Emergency Road Service coverage?

Emergency Road Service coverage can help you get moving again — fast — whether it's a tow or a roadside repair like a flat tire, a dead battery, or running out of fuel.

## 🚙 What is Rental Car and Travel Expenses coverage?

Rental Car and Travel Expenses coverage helps pay the cost of renting a replacement vehicle while your car is in the shop for a covered repair.

## 🔎 How is fault determined after an accident?

Liability, or fault, will be determined based on state laws and the circumstances of the accident. Depending on the facts of the loss, there may be shared responsibility between the parties involved.

## 💳 Who pays my deductible if I'm not at fault?

Your insurer will work to recover your deductible from the at-fault party or their insurance company, though recovery isn't guaranteed. If there is shared responsibility, the amount you receive back may be prorated based on the percentage of fault.

## 🔧 Do I have to get my car repaired after a claim?

The decision to repair is yours — and it's okay to not repair your vehicle. However, if a lienholder exists on your vehicle, they may require repairs. If you choose not to repair, your insurer will issue payment minus your deductible.

## 📣 Do I have to report an accident even if I'm not at fault?

You are required to report a claim even if it's not your fault. Reporting protects your interests, especially when injuries or property damage have occurred.

## 🕐 How long do car repairs take after a claim?

Many property damage claims are resolved within 7 to 14 days, but repair times can vary greatly based on your vehicle, the extent of the damage, and parts availability.

## 📈 Will filing a claim raise my premium?

Your claim will not impact your current rate. However, when you renew your policy, your rate may increase depending on the claim. Claims under $500 or claims that go unpaid typically don't raise rates.

## 🚗 What does it mean if my vehicle is a total loss?

Generally, a vehicle is a total loss when the cost to return it to its pre-loss condition is greater than the value of the vehicle. In some states, a vehicle may be considered a total loss if repair costs would exceed a percentage of the vehicle's value, such as 80%.

## 💵 How much will I receive for my total loss vehicle?

Your insurer pays you the actual cash value of your vehicle — the market value based on factors such as its pre-loss condition, age, options, and mileage — minus any applicable deductible.

## 📁 What paperwork is needed before a total loss payment is issued?

Your insurer typically needs the physical title with your signature and an odometer disclosure statement. If a lender holds the title, they obtain it after you complete a Power of Attorney and odometer statement. Additional state requirements may apply.

## 💰 Will I still owe money on my loan or lease after a total loss?

You may still owe money if the vehicle's actual cash value is less than your remaining loan or lease balance. Loan/Lease Payoff coverage, also known as gap coverage, can help cover the difference up to your policy limits.

## 🔄 Will my insurance automatically transfer to my replacement vehicle after a total loss?

No. You must log in, call, or contact your agent to remove the totaled vehicle from your policy and add your replacement vehicle. Most insurers allow you to add a replacement vehicle within 30 days of ownership.

## 🔩 What are my repair and inspection options after filing a claim?

Options typically include using a network repair shop recommended by your insurer, choosing any repair shop of your preference, or using a photo estimate tool through your insurer's mobile app to document damage remotely.

## 📊 What if my repairs cost more than the original estimate?

If additional loss-related damage is found during the repair process, your insurer will re-inspect your vehicle and update the estimate as needed to account for the additional covered damage.

## 🏆 Is there a guarantee on repairs made through my insurer's network?

If you choose one of your insurer's network shops, you may receive a lifetime guarantee on covered repairs for as long as you own or lease your vehicle. This guarantee covers defects in workmanship related to form, fit, finish, durability, and functionality, but excludes normal wear and tear, neglect, abuse, or damage from a subsequent accident.

## 🚘 How do I submit an auto insurance claim?

You can file a claim by logging in to your policy online, through your insurer's mobile app, or by calling the claims center. If you are not a policyholder, many insurers allow you to file a claim online as a guest after an accident.

## 🪟 How does the windshield and glass repair process work?

File your claim online, through the app, or by calling your claims center. A representative will explain your coverage and schedule the repair or replacement. Glass repairs typically take less than 30 minutes and replacements around 60 minutes. If your damage is repairable and you have comprehensive coverage, there may be no cost to you. A deductible may apply for full replacements depending on your state.

## ☁️ Does car insurance cover hail damage?

If your vehicle is damaged by hail, auto comprehensive coverage may help pay to repair or even replace your vehicle, minus your deductible. Parking your car in a garage or under a carport is the easiest way to prevent hail damage to your vehicle.

## 🔢 What does 100/300/100 mean in car insurance?

These numbers refer to liability coverage limits: up to $100,000 for bodily injury per person, up to $300,000 total for bodily injury per accident, and up to $100,000 for property damage per accident. Required minimum liability limits vary by state.

## 🆚 When should I choose liability-only versus full coverage?

Full coverage is a term used to describe policies that include liability, comprehensive, and collision coverage. If your car is older and paid off, liability-only coverage may be enough. If you have a newer vehicle, a loan or lease, or want added protection for your own car, adding comprehensive and collision coverage is typically the better choice.

## ⚡ What happens if my damages exceed my liability coverage limits?

If your damages exceed your liability coverage, you are personally responsible for paying the remaining amount out of pocket. This is why many drivers choose higher liability limits to better protect their assets.

## 🌐 Is liability car insurance required in all states?

Liability car insurance is required in most states. Only a few states, like New Hampshire and Virginia, allow drivers to operate vehicles without liability insurance, but they have alternative requirements such as proof of financial responsibility.

## 🚙 Does liability insurance cover rental cars?

Liability insurance typically covers rental cars, but only for damages or injuries you cause to others while driving the rental. It does not cover damage to the rental vehicle itself.

## 🏦 Can you have liability-only insurance on a financed car?

You can have liability insurance on a financed car, but most lenders will also require you to carry comprehensive and collision coverage to protect their financial interest in the vehicle. Failing to maintain this coverage may result in the lender purchasing insurance on your behalf at a higher cost.

## 🌂 What is a personal umbrella insurance policy?

A personal umbrella policy is a type of liability insurance that provides additional coverage beyond the limits of your homeowners, auto, or other personal insurance policies. It can cover a broader range of claims and may apply across multiple underlying policies.

## 👴 Should retirees consider umbrella insurance?

Yes. An umbrella insurance policy can protect retirees' savings and assets from large liability claims or lawsuits that exceed the limits of their existing home or auto policies.

## 🔍 What is an insurance adjuster and what do they do?

An insurance adjuster, also known as a claims adjuster, is a person who investigates an insurance claim to determine if the insurer should pay for damage or injuries, and if so, how much they should pay. The adjuster reviews claim details and policy coverage, interviews involved parties and collects statements, then coordinates vehicle inspections, writes repair estimates, and arranges repairs or provides claim payouts. For totaled vehicles, the insurer pays the actual cash value minus the deductible.

## 🚗 What is a typical car insurance coverage combination?

Many drivers carry liability car insurance coverage because it is required in most states. However, it can be a good idea to add optional coverages like collision, comprehensive, uninsured/underinsured motorist, and medical payments for more complete protection.
